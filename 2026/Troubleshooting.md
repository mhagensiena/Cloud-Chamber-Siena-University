<details>
<summary><b>Power Supply Won't Turn On</b></summary>

### Check These First

* Verify that **Green Wire 1** and **Black Wire 2** are properly soldered together.
* Make sure the solder joint is fully covered with electrical tape.
* Ensure no exposed wires are touching each other.
* Unplug and reconnect the 24-pin connector. This can act as a reset.
* Verify that the power switch on the rear of the power supply is in the **ON** position.

### Most Common Cause

> [!TIP]
> The most common cause is soldering the wrong pair of wires together. Double-check the wire labels and compare them to the instructions.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Fan Won't Spin</b></summary>

### Check These First

* Verify **Black Wire 1** is inserted into the far-left slot of the fan adapter.
* Verify **Yellow Wire 1** is inserted into the slot directly beside Black Wire 1.
* Confirm all lever nuts are fully closed.
* Check that all wires are securely seated.

### If the Problem Persists

* Remove and reinstall the wires in the lever nuts.
* Verify that the correct yellow and black wires were used.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Peltier Is Not Getting Cold</b></summary>

### Check These First

* Verify the red wire is connected to a yellow 12V wire.
* Verify the black wire is connected to a black ground wire.
* Check that the Peltier is installed with the correct orientation.
* Ensure thermal paste is applied evenly.
* Verify the Peltier is pressed firmly against both mating surfaces.

### If the Problem Persists

1. Disconnect the Peltier.
2. Perform the isolated test from Step 13.
3. Measure its temperature after several minutes.

> [!WARNING]
> If the Peltier cannot cool below room temperature during isolated testing, it is likely defective and should be replaced.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>LEDs Won't Light Up</b></summary>

### Check These First

* Verify **Yellow Wire 3** is connected to the positive LED terminal.
* Verify **Black Wire 5** is connected to the negative LED terminal.
* Check that the LED connector arms are fully seated on the copper pads.
* Ensure no exposed wiring is creating a short circuit.

### If Using Soldered Connections

* Inspect all solder joints.
* Reflow any dull or cracked joints.
* Verify solder is bonded to the copper pads and not merely resting on them.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>No Particle Tracks Visible</b></summary>

### Work Through These Steps in Order

#### 1. Wait Longer

The chamber may require 5–10 minutes to reach operating temperature.

#### 2. Verify Alcohol Quantity

* Use approximately **0.5 mL**.
* Spread evenly across the chamois cloth.

#### 3. Verify Alcohol Concentration

* Use **90% isopropyl alcohol or higher**.
* Lower concentrations often contain too much water.

#### 4. Check the Petri Dish Seal

* Verify the lid is fully seated.
* Ensure vapor is not escaping.

#### 5. Inspect Thermal Paste

* Remove the dish.
* Clean off old thermal paste.
* Apply a fresh, thin, even layer.
* Reinstall the dish.

#### 6. Verify the Radioactive Source

* Confirm the source is inside the dish.
* Ensure it is not blocked by any objects.

### Still No Tracks?

> [!TIP]
> Turn the chamber off, allow it to return to room temperature, reapply thermal paste, re-add alcohol, and restart the system. This resolves most track visibility issues.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Mist Is Visible But No Clear Tracks</b></summary>

### Possible Causes

* The chamber has not fully cooled yet.
* The lighting angle is not optimal.
* The petri dish bottom is too reflective.
* Condensation is reducing visibility.

### Solutions

* Wait several more minutes.
* Dim nearby room lights.
* Shine a flashlight across the chamber at a shallow angle.
* Verify the underside of the petri dish is painted black.
* Wipe the bottom surface with an alcohol-dampened cloth.

</details>

## Still Having Problems?

If none of the troubleshooting steps resolve the issue:

1. Turn the chamber off.
2. Disconnect power.
3. Inspect all wiring against the build guide.
4. Revisit the relevant assembly step.
5. Test each subsystem individually before reassembly.
